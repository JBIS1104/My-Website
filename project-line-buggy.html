Hardware

What’s inside.

Microcontroller — STM32F401RE (mbed)

The buggy is controlled by an STM32F401RE microcontroller running mbed. It manages all sensor ADC reads, PID computation, motor PWM output, encoder feedback, and Bluetooth communication with the HM-10 BLE module.

Sensor Array — 6× TCRT5000 IR Sensors (PCB)

Two iterations of a custom PCB sensor array were designed. The final version used a common-emitter configuration with 75 Ω current-limiting resistors and 4.7 kΩ output resistors, giving readings across the full 3.3 V ADC range. The board was arc-shaped to fit around the ball castor, allowing earlier detection of curves. Background subtraction (IR LED on minus IR LED off) was applied in software to eliminate ambient lighting interference. A sensor failure before heats reduced the working array from 6 to 4 sensors, creating blind spots between readings.

Chassis — Redesigned for Low CG & Compactness

The chassis was fully redesigned after TD2. The battery pack was repositioned between the gearboxes to lower and centralise the centre of mass, improving cornering stability. An upper strut-brace (inspired by the BMW M3 strut tower brace) links both motor mounts, creating a torsion-resistant structure while doubling as the motor-controller board mount. Plastic hex standoffs and screws replaced steel equivalents, saving approximately 80 g. Cutouts in the chassis base removed excess material without compromising structural load paths.

Drive & Power

Two DC gearbox motors are driven via a motor-controller board, with encoder feedback for closed-loop speed control. Non-rechargeable Duracell batteries were selected over rechargeables to ensure consistent voltage under load during motor tuning and heats runs.

Control Software — State Machine + PID

A state machine manages four states: line-following, line-lost, stopping, and turnaround. The weighted-average sensor position feeds a single PID controller with trapezoidal integration, anti-windup protection, and low-pass filtering. A Bluetooth-triggered 180° turnaround (via HM-10 BLE) was implemented using encoder-measured distance. Gains were tuned sequentially: P → D → I across multiple base speeds.

Outcome

Results.

The buggy performed well on the pre-heats test track, completing the course multiple times. However, a sensor failure before heats reduced the array from 6 to 4 sensors, creating blind spots that caused all three timed attempts to fail — losing the line after the turnaround (attempt 1), immediately at the first curve (attempt 2), and at the bottom curve after a successful ramp run (attempt 3). The team did not qualify for the final race, though the mechanical design, control algorithm, and most TD4 marks were secured.
